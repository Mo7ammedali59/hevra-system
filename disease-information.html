Rare Disease Information

Progeria (Hutchinson-Gilford Progeria Syndrome - HGPS)

Prevalence: 1 in 4-8 million
Description

A rare genetic disorder causing accelerated aging in children.

Symptoms
  • Growth delays
  • Hair loss
  • Aged-looking skin
  • Joint stiffness
  • Cardiovascular issues
Treatment

No cure; management includes medications like Zokinvy to reduce cardiovascular complications.

Cystic Fibrosis (CF)

Prevalence: ~40,000 in U.S.
Description

A genetic disorder affecting the lungs and digestive system due to thick mucus production.

Symptoms
  • Chronic cough
  • Frequent lung infections
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Digestive issues
Treatment

Airway clearance techniques, inhaled medicines, pancreatic enzyme supplements, and CFTR modulators.

Gaucher Disease

Prevalence: 0.9 per 100,000
Description

A genetic disorder where fatty substances accumulate in certain organs due to enzyme deficiency.

Symptoms
  • Enlarged liver and spleen
  • Bone pain
  • Anemia
  • Fatigue
Treatment

Enzyme replacement therapy and substrate reduction therapy.

Hemophilia

Prevalence: 12 per 100,000 males
Description

A bleeding disorder where blood doesn't clot properly due to missing clotting factors.

Symptoms
  • Prolonged bleeding
  • Spontaneous bleeding episodes
  • Joint damage
Treatment

Regular infusions of clotting factor concentrates.

Angelman Syndrome

Prevalence: 1 in 12,000-20,000
Description

A neuro-genetic disorder affecting the nervous system, often due to a missing or mutated UBE3A gene.

Symptoms
  • Developmental delays
  • Lack of speech
  • Seizures
  • Happy demeanor
Treatment

No cure; therapies focus on managing symptoms and improving quality of life.

Fibrodysplasia Ossificans Progressiva (FOP)

Prevalence: 0.88 per million
Description

A rare genetic disorder where soft tissues progressively turn into bone, leading to restricted movement.

Symptoms
  • Malformed toes at birth
  • Muscles and connective tissues ossify
  • Stiffness and immobility
Treatment

No definitive cure exists. Management focuses on symptom relief and preventing flare-ups. The drug palovarotene (Sohonos) has shown promise.

Xeroderma Pigmentosum (XP)

Prevalence: 1 in 1,000,000
Description

A genetic condition causing extreme sensitivity to ultraviolet (UV) rays due to defective DNA repair mechanisms.

Symptoms
  • Severe sunburns with minimal exposure
  • Dry skin
  • Freckling
  • High risk of skin cancers
Treatment

Strict UV protection measures, including protective clothing and sunscreens; regular dermatological assessments.

Porphyria

Prevalence: 1 in 10,000
Description

A group of disorders resulting from buildup of natural chemicals that produce porphyrin, essential for hemoglobin function.

Symptoms
  • Abdominal pain
  • Muscle pain
  • Seizures
  • Sensitivity to sunlight
Treatment

Avoidance of triggering factors, medications to reduce porphyrin levels, and supportive therapies.

Alice in Wonderland Syndrome (AIWS)

Prevalence: 16.5% of migraine patients
Description

A neurological condition causing distorted perception, where objects appear larger or smaller than they are.

Symptoms
  • Visual distortions
  • Altered body image
  • Time perception anomalies
Treatment

Addressing underlying causes like migraines or infections; no specific treatment for AIWS itself.

Proteus Syndrome

Prevalence: <1 in 1,000,000
Description

A rare condition characterized by overgrowth of bones, skin, and other tissues.

Symptoms
  • Asymmetric overgrowth leading to disfigurement
  • Skin lesions
  • Vascular anomalies
Treatment

Surgical interventions to manage overgrowth; supportive therapies.
